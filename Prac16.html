Mahendra Singh Dhoni, popularly known as MS Dhoni, is one of India's greatest cricketers and most successful captains. Born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, Jharkhand, Dhoni made his international debut in 2004 and quickly became known for his exceptional wicketkeeping and explosive batting. Under his leadership, India won the ICC T20 World Cup in 2007, the ICC Cricket World Cup in 2011, and the ICC Champions Trophy in 2013, making him the only captain to win all three major ICC trophies. Dhoni was known for his calm demeanor on the field, earning him the nickname "Captain Cool." He was also a master finisher in ODIs, famously sealing India's 2011 World Cup victory with an unforgettable six. In his 350-match ODI career, he scored over 10,000 runs at an average above 50, while also excelling as a wicketkeeper. Besides international cricket, Dhoni was instrumental in the Chennai Super Kings' success in the IPL, leading them to multiple titles. He retired from international cricket on August 15, 2020, but continues to play in the IPL. His leadership, cricketing intelligence, and ability to perform under pressure have cemented his legacy as one of the greatest cricketers of all time.Mahendra Singh Dhoni, popularly known as MS Dhoni, is one of India's greatest cricketers and most successful captains.
